Work and Experience

This page documents any jobs or positions I have held since graduating highschool in 2021. This page is listed in descending order by starting date.

Cyclone Steel Services

Intern, May 2024- July 2024

After my Junior year, I was an operations intern for Cyclone Steel Services, located in Houston, TX. I re-built their welding cost calculator over my 8 week internship, and conducted a S.W.O.T. analysis. In order to complete the welding calculator, I developed a time study to evaluate how it took to weld a cylinder based on material thickness and weld length. After working with the welders in the shop, we were able to devise a time sheet to keep track of the labor hours required for different cylinders. This information, along with material usage data, material pricing, and other factors created the final welding calculator. This calculator ultimately reduced the realized welding cost by an average of 50%.

Kappa Sigma Fraternity

Director of Alumni Relations, Jan 2024- May 2024

In the spring semester of my Junior year, I was elected as the Alumni Relations Chair. I was charged with rekindling our newsletter, which had not been sent in almost four years. I was also responsible for planning our fall alumni weekend to strengthen our network with our alumni. I managed a committee of 6 members and helped delegate tasks and set clear deliverables and due dates. Ultimately, we were able to effectively re-engage our alumni and help raise money for maintining and upgrading our property. Most recently, this included a kitchen remodel, of which alumni donated 33% of the total cost.

Kappa Sigma Fraternity

Director of Philanthropy, May 2023- December 2023

Every Fall our fraternity hosts Wicked Woods, our marque philanthropy event. We host a Haunted House and sell tickets to the Bryan / College Station community. 100% of our ticket and merchandise sales goes to two charities: The Kappa Sigma Military Heroes Campaign and the Brazos Valley Foodbank. I was responsible for the planning and execution of this event in the fall of my Junior Year. I coordinated our set-up schedule, sold sponsorships to help raise money for props and building materials, and ran our marketing plan. Despite cancelling half of our nights due to rain, we were able to raise $46,000 for charity, donating the most money to charity out of any fraternity for the fall semester. Holding this position was very challenging, but I learned the value of working for a team and the importance of communicating with team members to ensure the completion of a common goal. This was one of my most rewarding experiences as a college student.

Sigma Delta Honors Society

Professional Planning Officer, May 2023- May 2024

I was honored to be selected as an officer in the Sigma Delta Honors Society for the 2023-2024 academic year. As the professional planning officer, I was responsible for coordinating events with other Sigma Delta officers. In the fall semester, I helped plan our tailgate with SRS Distribution, one of our sponsors. In the spring semester, I planned our first annual Senior Dinner. This event serves as a send-off for our senior students and gives the organization a change to honor our graduating members and their accomplishments. Working as a Sigma Delta officer provided another opportunity to grow my skills as a teammate. Working with experienced officers, I had to be constantly communicating to ensure that every-aspect of our events were taken into consideration and planned. This helped me strengthen my communication skills and gave me a chance to improve my soft skills.

ProFormance Racing School

Instructor, May 2021- Current

As a sophomore in Highschool, I started racing cars with my father. After two years of practicing and competing at the local level, I interned with the ProFormance Racing School in Seattle, Washington for a senior project. I returned that summer as a seasonal instructor and have worked there every summer since. I manage 2-4 clients daily, many of whom have never driven on a race track before. I am charged with bridging the information from the classroom onto the track, and demonstrating high performance driving skills. Of the jobs I have had, it has been the most interesting. Unlike many of my other jobs, I am directly responsible for the safety of my clients. This includes their personal safety (i.e. ensuring they are hydrated and comfortable in the car) and vehicle safety (i.e. ensuring my clients cars are fit to drive on a race track). This gave me a lot of confidence to make decision on the job and that skill has transfered to every other job or position I have held.
